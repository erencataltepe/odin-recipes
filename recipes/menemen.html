Menemen

Description

Menemen is a popular traditional Turkish dish[3] which includes eggs, tomato, green peppers, and spices such as ground black and red pepper cooked in olive oil.

Menemen may be made with onions, but the addition of onions is often debated and is more common when menemen is eaten as a main dish, rather than at breakfast. The dish is similar to shakshouka.

Menemen is commonly eaten for breakfast and served with bread.[4] Its name originates from a small town in Izmir Province.[5]

Ingredients

  • 1 tablespoon extra-virgin olive oil
  • ¼ cup chopped onion
  • 2 tablespoons diced red bell pepper
  • 2 tablespoons diced Aleppo chiles, or to taste
  • ½ teaspoon red pepper flakes
  • ¼ cup chopped tomato
  • salt and ground black pepper to taste
  • 3 large eggs, beaten

Steps

  1. Heat olive oil in a skillet over medium heat. Add onion, bell pepper, chiles, and red pepper flakes. Cook and stir until onions are soft and translucent, 5 to 6 minutes. Add garlic and cook until fragrant, about 1 minute. Add tomatoes, with their juices, and season with salt and pepper. Cook until tomatoes are softened, 4 to 6 minutes.
  2. Add eggs to the skillet, tilting the skillet until eggs cover skillet completely. Do not mix. Cook until eggs are set, 3 to 5 minutes.
  3. Top with basil and feta cheese.
