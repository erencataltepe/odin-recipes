Sarma

Description

Zeytinyağlı Yaprak Sarma is a classic Turkish dish made by rolling grape leaves around a filling of rice, onions, herbs, and pine nuts, cooked gently in olive oil. It is often served cold as part of a meze spread.

Ingredients

  • 250 g grape leaves (fresh or jarred, rinsed)
  • 1 cup rice (short-grain)
  • 2 onions, finely chopped
  • 1/4 cup pine nuts (optional)
  • 1/4 cup currants (optional)
  • 1/2 cup fresh parsley, chopped
  • 1/2 cup fresh dill, chopped
  • 1/2 cup olive oil (plus extra for drizzling)
  • Juice of 2 lemons
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 1 tsp black pepper

Steps

  1. If using jarred grape leaves, rinse them under cold water to remove excess brine. If fresh, blanch them in hot water for a few minutes to soften.
  2. In a pan, heat half of the olive oil. Sauté onions until soft. Add pine nuts, rice, currants, salt, and pepper. Cook for 5 minutes, stirring. Remove from heat and mix in parsley and dill.
  3. Place one grape leaf on a flat surface, shiny side down. Add 1 tsp of filling near the stem end. Fold the sides in, then roll tightly.
  4. Line the bottom of a wide pot with extra grape leaves. Arrange the stuffed grape leaves seam-side down in layers.
  5. Pour remaining olive oil and lemon juice over the rolls. Add water to just cover them. Place a plate on top to prevent them from floating.
  6. Cover and cook on low heat for 40–50 minutes, until rice is fully cooked.
  7. Let cool and serve at room temperature, drizzled with olive oil and lemon.
