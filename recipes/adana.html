Adana Kebap

Description

Adana Kebab is a famous Turkish dish named after the city of Adana. It is made from hand-minced lamb meat, flavored with red pepper flakes and spices, and grilled on skewers over charcoal. It is usually served with flatbread, grilled vegetables, and onions.

Ingredients

  • 500g Lamb, finely minced
  • 1 onion
  • 2 cloves garlic
  • 1-2 tbsp red pepper
  • 1 tsp ground cumin
  • 1 tsp salt
  • Skewers

Steps

  1. In a large bowl, combine minced lamb, grated onion, garlic, red pepper flakes, cumin, salt, and black pepper.
  2. Knead the mixture well for 5–10 minutes until it becomes sticky and holds together.
  3. Wet your hands with water, then mold the meat mixture onto skewers, pressing it firmly.
  4. Preheat a charcoal or gas grill. Grill the kebabs for about 8–10 minutes, turning occasionally until fully cooked.
  5. Serve hot with flatbread, grilled peppers, tomatoes, and onions.
